# Allergen Information

## Common Allergens in Costco Products

### Dairy
Contains milk: Kirkland Whole Milk, Kerrygold Butter, Parmigiano Reggiano, all yogurts, ice creams, Kirkland Croissants (butter), Caesar Salad Kit (dressing, parmesan), Lasagna, Bakery Cookies, Bagels, Quaker Granola Bars (some).

### Gluten / Wheat
Contains wheat: All breads, bagels, croissants, cookies, animal crackers, Quaker bars, lasagna, frozen dumplings, all pastas.

### Nuts (Tree Nuts & Peanuts)
Contains nuts: All trail mixes, mixed nuts, almonds, peanut butter, almond milk, some granola bars, RXBARs. Manufactured in facility with nuts: many bakery items.

### Soy
Contains soy: Many sauces, marinades, some Bibigo dumplings, some snack bars, infant formulas (check label).

### Eggs
Contains eggs: Kirkland Organic Eggs, baked goods, croissants, pasta dishes, RXBARs.

### Fish & Shellfish
Contains fish: Salmon fillets, salmon burgers, albacore tuna. Note: shellfish (shrimp, crab) sold separately, not in standard inventory.

### Sesame
Contains sesame: Sesame bagels, hummus (tahini), some bread products.

## Critical Safety Notes for Agent

### Severe Allergies — ALWAYS Escalate
If customer mentions ANY of these, escalate to human:
- Peanut allergy
- Tree nut allergy
- Shellfish allergy
- Severe gluten / celiac
- Multiple food allergies
- "EpiPen" or "anaphylaxis"
- Child with allergy

Say: "For allergen safety, let me connect you with our member services team — they can pull the exact ingredient list and confirm cross-contamination risks. One moment."

### Cross-Contamination Risks
Costco bakery items (cookies, bagels, croissants, muffins) are made in shared facilities. Cross-contamination with nuts, dairy, eggs, wheat is possible. Never tell customer something is "safe" — always recommend they verify the label or escalate.

### Kirkland Signature Specific
Kirkland items have detailed ingredient labels on packaging. The agent can read from this allergen file but should always say "ingredient information current as of last update — please verify the label for any changes."

### Organic ≠ Allergen-Free
Organic items still contain allergens (organic eggs still have eggs, organic peanut butter still has peanuts). Don't conflate "organic" or "natural" with allergen safety.

### Dietary Filter Recommendations
- **Dairy-free options**: Kirkland Almond Milk, Kirkland Coconut Water, Kirkland Organic Hummus, fresh produce, plain meats, RXBARs, La Croix, sparkling water
- **Vegan options**: Same as dairy-free, plus check for honey, eggs, fish
- **Gluten-free options**: Kirkland Organic Quinoa, fresh produce, plain meats/seafood, Kirkland Mixed Nuts, hummus, salsa, RXBARs (verify label)
- **Kosher options**: Look for OU symbol on packaging (many Kirkland items)
- **Halal options**: Limited; check individual product labels

### When in Doubt
The agent should default to caution. If unsure about an allergen, say:
"I want to make sure we get this right — let me transfer you to a specialist who can verify the exact ingredients."
